The Veteran Readiness and Employment (VR&E) program is specifically designed to help veterans with service-connected disabilities get and keep a job. The VR&E program provides a number of benefits to employers who hire eligible veterans, including reimbursements that reduce the cost of training new employees. The VA can also help employers access federal tax credits for hiring veterans.

On-the-Job Training (OJT) Program

Under this program, the VA can pay a portion of the veteran's salary for up to 24 months while they receive training. As the veteran's skills and productivity increase, the employer's share of the salary gradually increases until the training is complete, and the veteran is fully integrated into the workforce.

By participating in the VR&E program, you not only help disabled U.S. Veterans but also receive valuable support and financial assistance to ensure their success.
